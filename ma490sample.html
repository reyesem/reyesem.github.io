Social Justice and Statistical Concepts

Home Teaching Research Consulting Miscellaneous

"Where there is an injustice, there is a need for social justice." Unfortunately, we live in a world which is broken, and that brokenness results in people being marginalized, overlooked, and/or oppressed. Data can be a weapon wielded to reinforce these patterns of injustice; or, it can tool for breaking them down and building a future which benefits everyone. A blend of both a statistical and humanities perspective, the course will discuss disparities within our society using a lens that incorporates data as well as the stories of the people that data represents.

Learning Objectives Course Structure

Learning Objectives

After completing this course, students will be able to accomplish the following tasks:

  1. Describe the importance of multivariable thinking in social contexts and apply a multivariable model to address a research question regarding social injustice.
  2. Identify the roles of data, history, culture, and other systems in creating and reinforcing inequity and injustice.
  3. Critique data collection and statistical analyses used to support arguments focused on equity and representation.
  4. Communicate clearly (verbally, textually, and visually) social justice concerns, statistical concepts, and their relationship to one another.
  5. Articulate the value and limitations of statistical thinking in discussions involving equity and representation.

Course Structure

This is a "seminar." Instead of weekly lectures, students will perform weekly readings; class time will be used to host discussions. Students entering the course should be prepared to engage with the readings and discussions each week.

The course will introduce statistical concepts as necessary to engage in the literature; however, a working knowledge of statistics at the introductory level is expected.
