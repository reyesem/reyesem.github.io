Introducing Water Footprints in an Introductory Statistics Course

Home Teaching Research Consulting Miscellaneous

During the 2023-2024 academic year, Rose-Hulman supported a network of faculty interested in injecting sustainability concepts throughout the curriculum. In collaboration with Jamie Reyes, we developed a lab for an introductory statistics course that allowed students to (a) practice skills learned in class associated with performing inference about the parameters of a regression model and (b) introduced students to the concept of virtual water and water footprints.

As a component of the lab activity, we asked students to state the amount of water (gallons) they believe is required to produce several common items. We then provided a short education on the amount of water required to produce one serving (4 oz) of apple. Students were then asked to repeat the activity. Below are the estimated water requirements (gallons) for those items:

Below are course materials associated with this lab.

  • Poster Presentation
    This poster summarizing the project was presented to other members of the sustainability network at Rose-Hulman.
  • Instructor Notes for Introducing Water Footprints
    The lab is introduced to students during class. This provides notes for the instructor on how to motivate the need for water conservation, and the need to characterize water use if we are to consider water conservation efforts.
  • Additional Background for Students
    The lab is introduced to students during class. This includes both a discussion of "virtual" water, water footprints, the utility of surveys, and the format of the lab report itself. Following the class discussion, this document is provided to students to support that discussion on water footprints.
  • Survey
    Each student completes a survey in class. The survey includes the following sections:
    • Demographic information: basic (non-identifying) demographic information is collected. This includes age and political affiliations that may be used to characterize water use.
    • Perceived water use: asks students to estimate their water use, describe some behaviors associated with water use, and estimate the water required to produce common products.
    • Education: a short paragraph is provided regarding "virtual" water in the production of apples. Participants are then asked to re-estimate the water required to produce common products (same set). This allows for investigation of the impact of the education on student perception of virtual water.
    • Water Footprint: participants are redirected to an online water calculator to estimate and then report their water footprint.
  • Instructions for Lab Report
    Students work in groups to address a question of interest using the available data. Each group is responsible for determining their research question, conducting an analysis, and presenting the results. The report consists of answering the attached questions. Note that these prompts are not specific to this particular context of water conservation. This report assesses the statistical skills learned in the course.
  • Student Reflection
    To assess the impact of the context of the lab on student perceptions regarding water conservation, students complete a reflection exercise. The attached questions were graded on completion. These align with the UN Sustainability Goals learning outcomes and internal Institute learning outcomes.
