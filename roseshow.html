Bill Kline Innovation Award Information

About the Award

Bill Kline exemplified humility, integrity, compassion, and hard work. He often stressed the importance of understanding the "big picture" to ensure value-creation. Recipients of the Bill Kline Innovation Award embody those qualities and are recognized for their ingenuity, innovation, ability to manage complex systems using a systems perspective and, most importantly, their project's overall impact on society.

  • Deadline: Submissions must be received by 11:59 PM on Wednesday, April 20.
  • All disciplines are encouraged to apply.
  • A $1000 cash prize accompanies the award (to be shared among all participanting team members).

Applying for the Innovation Award

Any project wishing to be considered for the Innovation Award should submit a short video summarizing their project:

  • (1 minute) Introduction to the team and the problem to be solved.
  • (2 minutes) Explanation of the project; this should be a high-level "elevator pitch" on the work completed which might, depending on your discipline, focus on how the technology works, what your device does, or the primary results of the research, for example.
  • (2 minutes) Description of how you managed the complexity of the project using a systems perspective and the project's overall impact on society; for non-engineering disciplines, a "systems perspective" might look like linking your work to the broader literature, describing considerations for various stakeholders, and placing the components of your work within a larger framework of your discipline.
  • (1 minute) Conclusion and future applicability of the project.

The above timing guidelines are recommendations; the video should be approximately 6 minutes in length.
