Interesting Statistics Links

  • SIBS Program: The Summer Institute for Training in Biostatistics (SIBS) program is a wonderful glimpse into the world of biostatistics. This program is intended for undergraduate students of any major with an interest in learning how statistics plays a role in the medical world. I attended this program in the summer of 2005 at North Carolina State University. This program played a large role in my decision to attend graduate school, study at North Carolina State University, and concentrate in biostatistics. In addition, this is where I met my wife!
  • CAUSEweb: This website has become a favorite. It is a great resource for statistics education. This includes access to activities, workshops, and articles regarding statistics education.
  • Isolated Statisticians: Known mostly for the listserv, this community of statisticians has been an abundant source of ideas for the classroom as well as tips on professional growth.
  • Conferences: There are some really great conferences out there. Here are a few of my favorite: JSM is the largest gathering of statisticians in one place. USCOTS is a conference on teaching statistics (held during odd years). eCOTS is the electronic version of USCOTS (held during even years); if you have an interest in remote education, this is a great conference to see examples of engagine students in a remote environment. Electronic Undergraduate Statistics Research Conference is aimed at undergraduate students with work presented by undergraduate students; held virtually, this is a great place to get a sense of what the statistics community has to offer.
